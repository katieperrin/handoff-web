## The Basic Idea

Renting out your designer bag works on the same principle as renting out a spare room on Airbnb. You own an asset that other people want temporary access to. Instead of it sitting unused, you make it available through a platform that handles logistics — authentication, shipping, insurance, matching with renters — and you earn money (or credits) for every period it's in circulation.

The key difference from peer-to-peer marketplaces is that the best platforms handle everything. You don't list the bag yourself, coordinate with strangers, ship it to individuals, or worry about what happens if someone spills coffee on your Birkin. The platform manages the entire lifecycle.

## How the Process Works

While each platform differs slightly, the general process for contributing a bag follows the same steps:

### Step 1: Submit Your Bag for Review

You start by telling the platform what you'd like to contribute. This typically means sharing photos and basic details: brand, model, color, approximate age, and current condition. Most platforms have a simple online form for this. At [The Handoffs](/for-bag-owners), for example, you submit through your member dashboard and a team reviews your submission within a few business days.

Not every bag will be accepted. Platforms maintain quality standards to protect their members' experience. Generally, bags need to be from recognized luxury houses (Chanel, Hermès, Louis Vuitton, Dior, Prada, Gucci, and similar), in good to excellent condition, and authentic. Heavy wear, stains, broken hardware, or structural damage will usually disqualify a bag.

### Step 2: Authentication and Intake

Once approved, you ship the bag to the platform. Most services provide a prepaid shipping label so it costs you nothing. When it arrives, the platform's authentication team inspects it — checking serial numbers, materials, hardware, stitching, and other markers of authenticity. This is a meaningful benefit even beyond the rental program: you get a professional authentication of your bag at no cost.

After authentication, the bag is photographed, cataloged, and added to the rental pool. Some platforms do light cleaning or conditioning at this stage as well.

### Step 3: Your Bag Enters the Pool

Once live, your bag becomes available for members to request. You don't need to do anything — no managing listings, no responding to inquiries, no trips to the post office. The platform handles all rentals, shipping between members, and returns.

### Step 4: You Earn

For every period your bag is available in the pool, you earn credits or cash. At The Handoffs, contributors earn [up to $60 in credits for every 30-day cycle](/for-bag-owners) the bag is in the pool. Credits apply directly to your own membership, which means your contributed bag can partially or fully offset what you pay to rent.

Over a year, a single bag earning $60 per cycle generates $720 in value — from a bag that was otherwise sitting on a shelf.

### Step 5: Recall Anytime

This is the part that matters most to bag owners: you stay in control. If you want your bag back for a vacation, a special occasion, or because you just miss it — you recall it. At The Handoffs, recalls are processed with no questions asked. The bag is returned to you, and you can re-contribute it whenever you're ready.

## What About Insurance and Damage?

This is the number one concern for bag owners, and rightly so. Handing a $10,000 bag to strangers requires serious trust. Here's how reputable platforms handle it:

**Insurance during transit:** Bags are insured while being shipped between the platform and renters. If something happens in transit, you're covered.

**Insurance during rental:** While a member has your bag, it's covered against damage. If a renter returns a bag with a new stain, scratch, or hardware issue, the platform covers the cost of repair or compensates you.

**Authentication protects against fraud:** Because every bag is authenticated and cataloged before entering the pool, there's no risk of a different bag being returned in its place.

**Condition monitoring:** Platforms inspect bags between rentals. Any wear beyond normal use is flagged and addressed before the bag goes out again.

At [The Handoffs](/faq), bags are fully insured throughout the rental cycle, and the platform handles all cleaning and maintenance between rentals. Contributors are never asked to pay for damage caused by renters.
